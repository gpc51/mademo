03. Made OS Solution
Repsol Perú + Made OS

Made OS is a multi-agent intelligence layer that connects to existing sensors and control systems. Integration in under 30 days, without halting operations. Permanently read-only: Made never writes to PLCs or SCADA.

01
Days 1–30
Integration and connection to critical assets
Priority assets are identified: PLEM, Terminal TM2, CDU and FCC units, process compressors, critical pumps, sulfur recovery system, and heat exchangers. Made OS connects to existing sensors (temperature, differential pressure, vibration, flow) via OPC-UA or plant API. By day 30, the refinery is visible in real time. Every signal has context, timestamp, and full traceability.
02
From implementation
Continuous analysis and baseline construction
Made OS learns the nominal behavior of each asset under different operating conditions: throughput, charge temperature, crude composition, cycle state. The AI agent classifies anomalies into criticality levels (Class A: major incident risk · Class B: operational degradation · Class C: informational alert). The first degradation patterns appear within the first weeks of operation.
03
From day 90 onwards
Continuous prescription toward zero incidents
Early detection of accelerated corrosion, out-of-range vibration, thermal degradation, heat exchanger fouling, and differential pressure deviations in lines. Made OS prescribes preventive actions to the maintenance team with actionable time windows, before progressive degradation becomes a failure. The conversational agent answers operational queries in real time. Objective: no integrity incidents, no unplanned downtime.
Real-time detection
24/7 continuous analysis of process signals. Alerts with intervention windows of hours to weeks before the event. No retrospective alerts.
Read-only, always
Made OS does not write or modify parameters in PLCs, DCS, or SCADA. Zero risk of operational interference. If Made is unavailable, operations continue exactly as before.
Repsol alert channel
WebApp, email, WhatsApp, or any endpoint defined by Repsol's IT/OT. Bidirectional conversational agent. Automatic executive reports for operations management.

07. Program Requirements

Made OS works on existing infrastructure. These three requirements determine whether a candidate asset qualifies for the program and which integration type applies.

1
Assets with available signals
At least one critical asset (PLEM, CDU, FCC, compressor, or piping system) with temperature, pressure, or flow sensors already in operation. Made does not install instrumentation from scratch. If the asset already has signals, the program can begin.
2
Coordinated OT + HSE + IT access
An Operations Manager or Maintenance Director with access to process data and authority to define the program's KPIs. Coordination with the IT/OT team for signal integration and with HSE for integrity assets. A 90-minute initial technical session with these three profiles is sufficient to get started.
3
Results validation commitment
At the end of the program, a 90-minute session with operations management to audit results against the initial baseline. Performance data and the ROI narrative belong to both parties. The final executive report is in audited format, ready to present to management.
Technical note · ATEX and IEC 60079 zones: The refinery operates with assets in potentially explosive atmospheres (Class I, Division 1 and 2 zones per NEC / ATEX Zone 1-2 per IEC 60079). Made's Edge AI hardware is IP67/NEMA 4X baseline. For assets in classified ATEX zones, we jointly evaluate the certified Ex enclosure before start; no equipment enters a classified zone without correct specification.
